The 2006 Chicago Bears' Playoff Run

Divisional Playoff NFC Championship Super Bowl

Bear Down

The Chicago Bears posted a 13-3 regular season record in 2006, the best in the NFC. Despite inconsistent quarterback play from Rex Grossman, the Bears were able to improve on the previous season's record of 11-5 and finished tied for second in the league in total points scored. The success of their season can be attributed to their elite defense, which was third in the league in points allowed, and to a breakout season from wide receiver Bernard Berrian. After earning a spot in the playoffs, the Bears fought their way to their first Super Bowl appearance since 1985.
